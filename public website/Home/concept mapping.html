Concept mapping is a statistical technique to unravel difficult constructs and to provide insight into complex problems. It is a relatively fast and efficient method which combines qualitative and quantitative techniques. There are six steps:

 

1.Preparation

During the preparation phase you formulate a seeding statement and you choose your participants.

 

2.Brainstorming

The brainstorming starts out with an individual task for participants. The seeding statement is a starting point for free association and participants are asked to generate as many ideas as possible concerning the seeding statement. This is followed by the nominal group technique, where participants share their ideas one by one. These ideas are called statements and each statement should be clear and should contain no more than one idea. These statements are collected in Ariadne.

 

3.Structuring

Participants individually get a rating and sorting task. All statements are presented and participants are asked how important this statement is. For the sorting task, participants are asked to sort the statements into groups that makes sense to them. Both tasks are done using Ariadne.

 

4.Data processing

Data processing is done by Ariadne, combining individual sorting data into a concept map. The concept map is the graphical representation of the group’s views. It provides an at-a-glance guide to the different aspects which participants recognized in the problem with which they were presented, and shows how those aspects are related.

To calculate this map Ariadne starts by counting how often participants have placed statements into a single group during the sorting task. The more often two statements are placed into the same group, the smaller the imaginary distance between them, which means that participants see a strong link between them in terms of content.

Next, Ariadne represents these imaginary distances visually on a two-dimensional graph, the so-called Concept Map. Statements often placed in the same group are projected close together; statements which are rarely or never linked by participants are shown far apart. Statements which lie close together on the map are combined to form clusters of related statements, using a cluster analysis, and a box is drawn around them.

Finally, Ariadne calculates the average rating for each statement and for each cluster, using the information from the rating task. The width of the line which defines the ‘cluster box’ indicates the relative importance which participants attached to each cluster.

 

5.Interpretation

Clusters and axes of the concept map are labelled.
